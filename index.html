Geotag Editor FAQs

Common questions about our GPS photo editing tool answered by the GeotagEditor.com team.

How do I add GPS coordinates to old photos?

Our geotag editor tool allows you to add location data to any JPEG image. Simply upload your photo, enter the latitude and longitude coordinates, and process the image. The GPS metadata will be embedded in the image file. It's perfect for adding location data to old vacation photos or archival images.

Is my privacy protected when using Geotag Editor?

Yes! Our geotag editor works 100% in your browser. Your images are never uploaded to any server. All processing happens locally on your device, ensuring complete privacy and security. This is why GeotagEditor.com is trusted by privacy-conscious users worldwide.

What image formats are supported?

Currently, we support JPEG/JPG files. These are the most common image formats that support EXIF metadata including GPS location data. Other formats like PNG or WebP don't support EXIF data, which is why we focus on JPEG for geotag editing.

Can I remove GPS data from my photos?

Yes! To remove location data, simply upload your image to our geotag editor and clear the coordinate fields before processing. This will create a new image file without GPS metadata, perfect for sharing photos online while protecting your privacy.

How accurate are the GPS coordinates I can add?

You can enter coordinates with up to 6 decimal places, which provides accuracy within approximately 10 centimeters. You can also click directly on the map for precise location selection. Our geotag editor gives you professional-level precision for all your location editing needs.
